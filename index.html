Scoring Format

Game Against the Median

Lets see how records and standings change with the extra game against the median.

Just to clarify before continuing whats happening below, Im computing the median of each weeks points for (remember for a data set with an even number of data points, median is equal to the average of the middle two points; for our 10-team league, that means the 5th and 6th highest-scoring teams), comparing each managers points for to the median, and awarding them a win if they are above it and a loss if they are below it.

Sleepers documentation specifies that in the rare case a manager scores exactly the median score, they will be awarded a tie, just in case anyone was worried about that.

We can already see some differences between this schedule and our head-to-head matchups. Lets put it all together and see how everyones record would change and what the standings would look like (the plot below is ordered by most total wins):

My lord. Its a whole different ballgamekind of. ettubrys still where he was, and robmitch28 and vekin8791 are still hanging at 6 and 7, but our top 4 shift around. We lose Scythe911 to mbf1808, and theres a shakeup in the ordering of the top 3 as well as a swap of JMDickson and TheChamp5796 between 8th and 9th. So as different as a ballgame can get when theres only about 10 pieces to move around and we already kind of deduced from max points and so on who might have benefitted from scheduling luck etc

But there you have it. Seems like there are only a few people who would benefit or lose standing from game against the median as of week 12.
